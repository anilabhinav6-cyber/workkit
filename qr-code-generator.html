How to use QR codes effectively

For businesses and restaurants

Replace printed menus with a QR code linking to your online menu. Update prices without reprinting. Hotels use QR codes on room cards to link to WiFi credentials, local guides, and room service menus — saving thousands on printing costs.

For marketing and advertising

Add a QR code to print ads, billboards, and packaging that links to your website, product page, or special offer. Track scan analytics by using a shortened URL (like bit.ly) as the QR destination.

For personal use

Create a QR code for your personal WiFi and frame it near your router — guests scan it instead of asking for the password. Or create a QR code for your WhatsApp to add to your business card.

Pro tip: Always test your QR code by scanning it before printing. Print at least 2cm × 2cm in size. Keep the white border (quiet zone) around the code — removing it stops the code from scanning correctly.
