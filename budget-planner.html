How to make a monthly budget

A monthly budget is a plan for how you'll spend your money each month. It starts with your total income and allocates amounts to every spending category — housing, food, transport, savings, and more. The goal is to ensure your expenses don't exceed your income, and that you're saving a meaningful amount each month.

Using our planner is simpler than a spreadsheet. Add your income sources, then fill in each expense category. The live summary shows your balance and savings rate instantly, and the visual bar shows exactly where your money goes.

Frequently asked questions

50% of income goes to needs (rent, food, utilities, transport), 30% to wants (dining out, shopping, entertainment), and 20% to savings and debt repayment. It's a simple, flexible guideline that works for most income levels.

Financial advisors generally recommend saving at least 20% of your monthly income. If that's not possible, start with 5–10% and increase gradually. Even saving AED 500/month invested at 7% annually grows to over AED 300,000 in 20 years.
