How FD interest is calculated

Indian banks compound fixed deposit interest quarterly. The maturity formula:

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

Where P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compounding frequency (4 for quarterly), t = years. A ₹1,00,000 FD at 7% for 5 years matures to approximately ₹1,41,478 — earning ₹41,478 in interest.

FD vs SIP — which is better?

FDs offer guaranteed, fixed returns (currently 6–7.5% at major banks) with zero market risk — ideal for capital protection. SIPs in equity funds historically return more (10–14%) but fluctuate with markets. Most planners suggest FDs for short-term goals and emergency funds, SIPs for long-term wealth.
