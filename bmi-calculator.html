Important: BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Athletes often have high BMI despite being very healthy. Consult a healthcare professional for a complete assessment.

Frequently asked questions

BMI is a useful screening tool but has limitations. It may overestimate body fat in muscular individuals and underestimate it in older adults. It also does not distinguish between visceral and subcutaneous fat.

The WHO defines 18.5–24.9 as healthy weight for most adults. However, BMI ranges for people of Asian descent are slightly lower — some guidelines use 18.5–22.9 as healthy for this group.

Multiply kilograms by 2.20462 to get pounds. Example: 70 kg × 2.20462 = 154.3 lbs. Use our unit converter for more conversions.
