How PDF compression works

PDF files are often bloated by high-resolution embedded images. This tool re-processes those images at a lower resolution and quality level, which is where most of the size reduction comes from. Text and vector content stay sharp. Compression results vary — image-heavy PDFs (scans, brochures) shrink dramatically, while text-only PDFs that are already small may barely change.

When to compress a PDF

Email attachments usually cap at 20–25 MB. Government and job-application portals often limit uploads to 2–5 MB. Compressing lets you meet those limits without deleting pages. Because everything happens on your device, even confidential documents like bank statements and contracts are safe to compress here.
